Introduction to Origami

Origami, Japanese for "paper folding", is the art of tranforming a square sheet of paper into a sculpture of another object through various folding techniques. If the paper is cut or glue is used, the method is refered to as kirigami. Origami refers to both the practice and the finished product. Folds are usually accomplished with any combination of one's fingers, a flat surface such as a table, and special creasing tools made out of bone.

Types of Paper

Printer Paper

Pros

  • widely available
  • thin and easy to fold

Cons

  • low quality
  • rectangular

Cardstock

Pros

  • strong material
  • nice texture

Cons

  • thick and stiff paper is harder to fold
  • not always a square

Sticky Notes

Pros

  • widely available
  • already a square
  • can be stuck to a flat surface to make initial folds easier

Cons

  • the sticky parts can get in the way of folding sometimes

Origami Paper

Pros

  • high quality
  • already a square
  • colorful

Cons

  • more expensive
  • less common
